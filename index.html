The Unpredictable Weather: Journey From Determinism to Chaos

Anubhab Mukherjee | 02 June 2026 | 15 min read

#Science

In the morning news, it was forecasted that it would be a sunny day. Hearing that you planned for a trip with your friends. But in the middle of your journey a torrential rain lashed out all your hope. Naturally all your grievances would fall on the news channel and on those meteorologists who you think are the most incompetent.

This is a very familiar scenario for the 20th and early 21st century people, who went through these mendacious weather reports. Today the situation has much improved. Now we can predict the weather for upcoming 15 days with good precision, albeit in most of the cases you can still see a drastic change in the weather data for the days 10 to 15, if you check it daily. Thus, the struggle is ongoing. I know, it's very obvious to raise the point that while scientists are advancing in the new era of artificial intelligence, we are still struggling with predicting the weather. Why so? Why does this 15-day barrier still exist? Is it the incompetence of our meteorologists? Or, lack of knowledge in weather study? To find a precise answer to these queries we need to go back to the Newtonian time, when we had just come to know that it is the same universal force, “Gravitation”, which was governing the fall of an apple from a tree, along with the planetary motions.
